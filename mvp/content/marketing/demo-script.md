# Demo script — 2 minutes flat

Use for: investor demos, partner demos, press demos. Same script, different framing in opening line.

Total time: 2:00. Practice it to under 1:45 so you have buffer.

---

## 0:00 — Hook (10s)

> "When I show up to the wrong bar for a big match, I want to throw my phone. Generic searches don't tell you where the Brazilians are, where the room sings, where the seats are open. GameDay Map fixes that — for the biggest tournament ever held."

(Pull up gamedaymap.com on screen.)

## 0:10 — The home (15s)

> "17 host cities. 48 nations. 104 matches. We tag every venue by which country's crowd actually shows up. Tap a city—"

(Click NYC.)

> "—and you see every bar and restaurant worth knowing. 74 today, hundreds incoming."

## 0:25 — The map (20s)

(Hover the map.)

> "Color-coded by venue type and country affiliation. I want a Brazil bar—"

(Click Brazil filter.)

> "—and the map collapses to where Brazil shows up. I want one with reservations available—"

(Toggle Reservations filter.)

> "—and it gets sharper. The free tier filters by 2 countries. Fan Pass at $4.99/month opens all 48."

## 0:45 — Tonight (15s)

(Click Tonight.)

> "On match days, this is the page that pays for itself. Today's matches, your top spots per match, kickoff in your timezone, save to calendar. Match-day email alerts for Fan Pass; push notifications for Elite at $12.99."

## 1:00 — A venue (15s)

(Click a venue.)

> "Capacity, atmosphere, country tag, address, reservations, directions. One tap to reserve. Elite includes a real human handling reservations on your behalf — Concierge — for groups of 6+."

## 1:15 — The business (25s)

> "Three tiers. Free is the funnel. Fan Pass at $4.99 is the workhorse — most fans buy this. Elite at $12.99 is for the crew leader and travel-group buyer. Subscription only. No ads. No data sales. We project most of the revenue comes from Fan Pass during the tournament window with retention through subsequent qualifiers and tournaments."
>
> "Two other revenue lines. Featured placements for venues — five per neighborhood at $99/month. And affiliate revshare — kit, tickets, ride share, OpenTable / Resy / Tock."

## 1:40 — The wedge (10s)

> "The wedge is country-supporter affiliation as a first-class filter. Yelp and Google can't ship that — they treat venues as restaurants. We treat them as fan rooms."

## 1:50 — Ask (10s)

> "Ask: {{specific_ask}}. Either way, kickoff is {{days_to_kickoff}} days away. Try it at gamedaymap.com."

## Variant: 5-minute demo (for partner / investor deeper dive)

Add the following segments between Tonight (0:45) and the venue tour (1:00):

- **Country page** (30s): "Every nation gets a curated guide — supporter culture, signature watch experience, top venues across all 17 cities. SEO machine."
- **Group booking** (30s): "Crew leader picks the bar, generates a /groups/code URL, friends RSVP, capacity is held — Elite-only. This is where the $12.99 tier earns it."
- **Submit a venue** (20s): "Community sourcing. Bar owners claim listings free. Fans add the spots we missed. Every submission is human-reviewed."
- **Admin dashboard** (40s): "Real-time view of submissions, leads, conversion funnel, top-saved venues, top-searched countries. We watch this like a heart monitor through the tournament."

## Voice and tone notes

- Don't apologize. Don't say "kind of," "we tried," or "we hope." Say what it does.
- Don't read the screen. Show, don't read.
- Pause for one beat after every CTA click — let them watch the page render.
- If they ask a question, answer in one sentence and continue. Save deep dives for after the demo.
- End on the ask. Always.

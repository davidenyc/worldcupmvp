## Slide 4 — Wedge and moat

**Title:** Why we win the room and Yelp can't.

**Punch:** Country-supporter affiliation is a first-class filter, not an afterthought.

**Body:**
- Yelp, Google, OpenTable treat venues as restaurants. We treat them as fan rooms.
- Country tagging is built from a combination of bar names, supporter-club partnerships, review mining, and human review. Hard to copy at scale without our editorial bench.
- Editorial picks (Hot Spot badges, Top 5 lists) are independent of paid placement — sponsorship cannot buy editorial slot. That trust is the long-term moat.
- The data flywheel: every saved venue, every reservation, every group booking sharpens the picks.
- Independent and subscription-funded — no investor pressure to add ads or sell data, which competitors with ad-supported models cannot match.

## Anticipated questions and short answers

**"Why isn't this a Yelp feature?"**
Country-supporter tagging requires editorial work that doesn't scale on a platform built around restaurant categories. Even if Yelp added the tag, fans don't trust it because Yelp's listings include sponsored spots in editorial slots. Trust is the wedge.

**"What happens after the World Cup ends?"**
Same product, different tournaments. Premier League, La Liga, Champions League, Copa America, Euros, AFCON, CONCACAF qualifiers — every cycle is a usage cycle. The "World Cup 2026" framing is the launch hook, not the lifespan.

**"Why won't the Fan Pass churn after the tournament?"**
Same reason gym memberships persist. The user identifies as the kind of person who watches matches in the right rooms. Annual plans at 35% off lock in the tournament-window users. We expect 30–40% net retention twelve months out — conservative for a sports-media subscription.

**"Why subscription instead of ads?"**
Ads create perverse incentives — we'd be optimizing for clicks, not the right room. Subscription aligns the product with the user. It also lets us decline obviously bad partner placements without revenue pressure.

**"What does scale require?"**
Editorial bench: 1 part-time city editor per 3 cities to keep listings sharp. Tech: a team of 2–3 engineers. Capital: enough to run for 18 months without revenue pressure during the post-tournament dip.

**"Defensibility against a venture-backed copycat?"**
Curated editorial bench + community submissions + venue partnerships built before the tournament. By the time a copycat ships, we've claimed the category. We're competing on trust, not features.

**"What's the worst case?"**
Worst case is small: tournament window underperforms, we cut burn, run lean through 2027 building the multi-tournament product. Subscription revenue + Featured placements covers a small team indefinitely.

**"What's the best case?"**
Best case is enormous: the watch-party-finder category for global football, reusable for any major sport, expandable internationally. The 2030 World Cup runs in Spain/Portugal/Morocco — same playbook, different cities.

# New York / New Jersey — what watch parties feel like here

If the World Cup has a spiritual home in North America for this tournament, it's the NY/NJ metro. MetLife Stadium hosts eight matches including the final, and every diaspora that exists has a room here. The trick isn't finding a bar — it's finding the *right* room for *your* match.

## How to think about NYC

Pick the country first. Then the borough. Then the bar. The room shifts dramatically across boroughs even for the same match. The Brazilian crowd in Astoria feels different from the Brazilian crowd in Newark feels different from the Brazilian crowd at Emporium Brasil in Midtown. None is more authentic — they're three different rooms.

For non-diaspora picks, default to a sports bar with multiple screens (Hole In The Wall locations, Smithfield, Banter, Football Factory) and call ahead — these fill up.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Brazil** — Emporium Brasil, Via Brasil (Midtown West); the "Little Brazil" stretch of W 46th. Newark Ironbound for a different vibe.
- **Argentina** — Palermo Bistro, Parrilla Steak House, Buenos Aires (Manhattan). Hudson County NJ has a quieter scene.
- **Mexico** — Cantina Rooftop, Cantina Taqueria locations, La Cantina. Sunset Park (Brooklyn) and East Harlem are the rooms with no English on the TV.
- **France** — Le Jardin Bistro, Brasserie Cognac. Smaller and tighter than the country's profile suggests.
- **Germany** — Radegast Hall (Williamsburg), Loreley (LES), Heidelberg (UES), Bierhaus (Midtown), Jägerhaus.
- **Spain** — the Boqueria locations (Soho, Flatiron, W40th), Tomiño, EZ Paella & Tapas.
- **Portugal** — Newark Ironbound is the room. Taste of Portugal, Tasca Portuguesa in Manhattan.
- **Italy** — not playing this World Cup. Italian-American bars will still show.
- **Korea Republic** — Koreatown 32nd Street: Seoul BAP, Soju Haus, Pocha 32, Itaewon Pocha, Gunbae, JuDam. Flushing Queens for another scene.
- **Japan** — Sozai (Izakaya Ramen), Izakaya Juraku, Izakaya NoMad, Izakaya Zest. East Village clusters.
- **IR Iran** — Persepolis (UES), Shiraz Kitchen & Wine Bar.
- **Jordan** — Levantine and other Lebanese/Levantine restaurants in Astoria and Manhattan.
- **Senegal** — Harlem and the Bronx — Sénégalais and Soninké restaurants. Le Baobab is a known spot.
- **Haiti** — Brooklyn (Flatbush, East Flatbush) and Queens (Cambria Heights). Tap Tap and similar.
- **Ecuador** — Jackson Heights (Queens) and Newark. Specific bars, ask locals.
- **Colombia** — Jackson Heights (Queens) is the densest Colombian neighborhood in the US.
- **England / Scotland** — Jones Wood Foundry, Smithfield Hall, Banter, The Soccer Republic locations. Boddingtons-on-tap pubs.
- **Netherlands** — Dutch Fred's, The Dutch (Soho).
- **Czechia** — Bohemian Hall & Beer Garden (Astoria) is the only real answer.
- **Belgium** — Belgian Beer Bar.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing for match days

- **Astoria, Queens** — Greek, Czech, Egyptian, Brazilian, Arab. The most diverse single neighborhood for diaspora watch parties.
- **Jackson Heights, Queens** — Colombian, Ecuadorian, South Asian.
- **Sunset Park, Brooklyn** — Mexican, Chinese.
- **Williamsburg / Greenpoint, Brooklyn** — Polish (not playing), German, generic sports bars.
- **Newark Ironbound, NJ** — Portuguese, Brazilian, Spanish. Cheaper than Manhattan and lights up for any of those countries.
- **Hudson County, NJ** — Argentine-Uruguayan steakhouses, Latin American.
- **Harlem and the Bronx** — Senegalese, Ghanaian, Dominican.

## Match-day logistics

- **Getting to MetLife:** NJ Transit train from Penn Station to Secaucus, then dedicated MetLife shuttle train. Allow 90 minutes door-to-door. Buses also run from Port Authority. No casual driving — parking is a nightmare on match days.
- **Watching from Manhattan when MetLife is hosting:** every venue near the Lincoln Tunnel and Penn Station gets crowded with the post-train spillover. Bars 8+ blocks east stay manageable.
- **Times Square specifically:** avoid for serious watching. Tourist crowd, not supporter crowd, even with the match on.
- **Fan zones:** check official FIFA fan zones at MetLife and likely outdoor zones in Manhattan parks (Bryant Park has hosted in the past).

## June/July weather

Hot and humid. Outdoor seating is a real factor. Many of the German beer halls have outdoor space — useful for afternoon matches, brutal for sustained heat. The NYC Persian and Levantine spots often have nice patios. Air conditioning at sports bars is reliably aggressive.

## Booking advice

- Marquee Saturday/Sunday matches: reserve at least 5 days out for groups of 6+.
- Weeknight afternoon kickoffs: walk-in friendly until quarterfinals.
- Final and semifinals: book within 24 hours of qualifying — every Manhattan bar with a country tag will sell out.

# Los Angeles — what watch parties feel like here

Los Angeles is the most diverse host city for this tournament. SoFi hosts eight matches including the USA opener. Every immigrant community in LA has its own room, and they're rarely in the same neighborhood as the nearest Hollywood sports bar. Pick the country and drive.

## How to think about LA

Two principles: distances are real, and the right room is often a restaurant, not a bar. The Mexican fan watching Mexico-Korea isn't at a Hollywood sports bar — they're at a cantina or carnitas spot in East LA, Boyle Heights, or San Fernando Valley. The Korean fan isn't there either — they're in Koreatown.

Plan an hour of driving each way for any match outside your neighborhood. Lyft/Uber prices spike around SoFi for ~3 hours pre/post.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Mexico** — Boyle Heights, East LA, San Fernando Valley. Cantina-style restaurants with the match on every screen and music between halves.
- **USA** — every sports bar in West LA, Santa Monica, downtown, Hollywood. SoFi adjacent neighborhoods on USA match days.
- **IR Iran** — Westwood ("Tehrangeles"). Persian restaurants and tea houses become packed match-day rooms. Cafe Glace, Shaherzad, Shamshiri.
- **Korea Republic** — Koreatown (Wilshire). Quarters Korean BBQ, soju bars, KBBQ-with-screens spots.
- **Japan** — Little Tokyo (downtown), Sawtelle (West LA), Torrance (South Bay).
- **Brazil** — Culver City and Mid-Wilshire have Brazilian restaurants; Bossa Nova locations show.
- **Argentina** — sparse, but San Pedro and the Westside have Argentine parrillas.
- **France** — French expat scene small but tight. Hollywood and Beverly Hills.
- **Germany** — Wirtshaus, Red Lion (Silver Lake) for German beer halls.
- **England / Scotland** — Cock 'n Bull (Santa Monica), Olde Ship (Long Beach + Fullerton), Grafton (Hollywood). UK pub network is robust.
- **Australia** — Aussie expat pubs in Venice and Santa Monica.
- **Spain** — Bodega LA, Otoño, La Espanola Meats.
- **Senegal / West African** — South LA pockets, supporter clubs in Inglewood and South LA.
- **Ethiopian / Eritrean** (not playing but proxy for African watching) — Little Ethiopia (Fairfax) shows African matches.
- **Saudi Arabia / Jordan / UAE** — Glendale and parts of Westwood.
- **South Africa** — small but tight, Manhattan Beach and Pasadena clubs.
- **Egypt** — Anaheim and the OC have small Egyptian-American clusters.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Tehrangeles (Westwood)** — the largest Persian community outside Iran. The IRN match is a Westwood event.
- **Koreatown** — every block has a screen, every BBQ spot will show. KOR matches are Koreatown events.
- **Boyle Heights, East LA, San Fernando Valley** — Mexican neighborhoods that go all-in for any Mexico match. Pocho and supporter cantinas.
- **Long Beach / Cambodia Town** — Asian community concentrated, will show JPN, KOR, others.
- **Westside (Santa Monica, Venice)** — Aussie, English, expat pubs. SoFi-adjacent on match days.
- **Inglewood** — SoFi neighborhood, will be the fan-zone hub on match days. Local Black-owned bars increasingly hosting USA, Senegal, Ghana.

## Match-day logistics

- **Getting to SoFi:** Metro K Line + free shuttle. Driving means $80+ parking and 2-hour exits. Plan accordingly.
- **From the eastside (Boyle Heights/Pasadena):** plan 90 min to SoFi.
- **From the Valley:** plan 90 min to SoFi.
- **Watching from K-Town when SoFi is hosting USA-Paraguay:** Koreatown sports bars are prime — close to downtown, lots of screens, easy parking compared to Westside.
- **Hollywood Bowl-style fan zones:** check FIFA's official LA fan zone (will likely be in DTLA or Inglewood).

## June/July weather

Hot, dry. Patios are usable in the morning and evening; brutal mid-afternoon. Many Westside venues have outdoor TVs. Marine layer keeps Santa Monica cooler than the Valley.

## Booking advice

- USA matches at SoFi: every Westside and SoFi-adjacent bar fills 4+ hours before kickoff. Reserve.
- Mexico matches: Boyle Heights and East LA cantinas don't take reservations but lines form 2 hours pre-kickoff. Or go to a sit-down restaurant in El Sereno that does take reservations.
- IR Iran matches in Tehrangeles: walk-ins only at most spots; arrive at first whistle for a seat.
- Marquee Korean matches: Koreatown KBBQ bars fill — book the larger places that take reservations.

## Submit a venue

We're particularly thin on Salvadoran, Guatemalan, Senegalese, and South African rooms in LA. If you know one, submit.

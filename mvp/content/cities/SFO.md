# San Francisco / Bay Area — what watch parties feel like here

Levi's Stadium in Santa Clara hosts six matches. The Bay's strength is its concentrated immigrant communities — Iranian in the Peninsula, Mexican in the Mission, Salvadoran in Daly City, Korean in Oakland and South Bay, Persian and Afghan in Fremont. Distances are real; pick the country first.

## How to think about the Bay

The Bay has three centers: SF proper (smaller, denser, fewer cars), the Peninsula/South Bay (sprawling, where most diaspora communities cluster), and the East Bay (Oakland/Berkeley, mixed). Plan for 45–90 min between any two for match day.

## Where each crowd lives

- **USA** — every sports bar in SoMa, Mission, Marina, downtown SJ. Kezar Pub, Final Final.
- **Mexico** — Mission District (24th St., Valencia), East Oakland, Fruitvale. Cantinas with screens.
- **Salvador / Central American** — Daly City, parts of San Mateo, East Oakland. Will show LATAM matches generally.
- **IR Iran** — Peninsula (San Ramon, Fremont, Pleasanton). Persian restaurants and tea houses.
- **Afghanistan / Central Asian** (proxy for IRN/UZB) — Fremont's Little Kabul. Massive.
- **Korea Republic** — Oakland's K-Town and Santa Clara/Cupertino. Wassada, Bowl'd.
- **Japan** — Japantown SF, Cupertino, San Jose. Izakayas and Japanese pubs.
- **China / Taiwan** — not playing, but Chinatown SF and Cupertino bars often show.
- **England / Scotland** — Mad Dog in the Fog (Lower Haight), Pig & Whistle, Royal Exchange. UK-pub scene small but loyal.
- **Brazil** — Brazilian Cultural Center (Mission), churrascarias in San Jose.
- **Argentina** — small; Argentine bistros in San Mateo and Mountain View.
- **Germany** — Schroeder's (FiDi), Suppenkuche (Hayes Valley).
- **Spain** — Zarzuela, Bocadillos.
- **Netherlands** — sparse.
- **Senegal / West African** — Bayview, Berkeley, Oakland small but tight.
- **Cabo Verde** — small pockets in Hayward and Oakland.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **The Mission** — Mexican and Latin American watching central. La Taqueria, El Farolito, El Toro on match days, plus pure cantinas.
- **Fremont (Little Kabul)** — Persian, Afghan, sometimes Uzbek matches will land here.
- **Oakland K-Town (telegraph corridor)** — Korean matches.
- **Cupertino / San Jose** — diverse Asian-American suburbs. Multiple flag bars.
- **Daly City** — Filipino-American (PHL not playing, but diaspora often watches Asian matches).
- **Hayes Valley / SoMa** — generic sports bars and craft-beer rooms.

## Match-day logistics

- **Levi's Stadium:** Caltrain to Mountain View, transfer to VTA light rail to Levi's. About 90 min from SF. Driving from SF is brutal.
- **From SF to Levi's by car:** budget 90 min for arrival, 2 hours for exit.
- **From East Bay:** BART to Fremont, then drive or use match-day shuttle if available.
- **Watching in SF on a Levi's match day:** SoMa and Mission are best; Marina fills with Stadium-bound traffic.

## Weather

SF cool and foggy; Santa Clara warm and dry. Big swings between SF (60s) and South Bay (80s+). Pack a layer for evening.

## Booking advice

- USA matches: SoMa and Mission sports bars fill; reserve.
- IRN match: Fremont restaurants don't take reservations for casual visits but the larger Persian restaurants (Ravagh, Maharaja) do.
- Mexico matches at Levi's or SoFi: Mission cantinas walk-in only and packed.
- Final/semifinal: every sports bar in SF and SJ books out. Reserve same-day-of-qualifying.

## Submit a venue

Salvadoran, Afghan, Senegalese, and Cape Verdean rooms in the Bay are underrepresented. We want them.

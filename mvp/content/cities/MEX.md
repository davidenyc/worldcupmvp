# Mexico City — what watch parties feel like here

Estadio Azteca hosts the opening match (Mexico-South Africa) plus four others. CDMX is the most football-crazed host city in the tournament, period. Every cantina, every pulquería, every taquería with a TV is going to be standing-room for any Mexico match.

## How to think about CDMX

For Mexico matches: pick a neighborhood and walk. Every spot will be packed and every spot will be loud. For non-Mexico matches: the cosmopolitan neighborhoods (Roma, Condesa, Polanco) will show; cantinas in working-class neighborhoods will pick the more popular fixtures.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Mexico** — everywhere. Cantinas in Centro, Coyoacán, Roma. Pulquerías. Taquerías. The roar from a Mexico goal carries across the city.
- **Argentina** — Polanco, Condesa, Roma. Argentine-style steakhouses (parrillas argentinas) and cafés.
- **Brazil** — small Brazilian community concentrated in Polanco and Roma.
- **Spain** — Spain has the deepest cultural ties; many Spanish-themed restaurants in Roma and Centro.
- **Colombia / Venezuela / Ecuador** — Latin American expat scenes in Roma and Centro.
- **USA / Canadian expats** — Roma Norte, Condesa, Polanco. American sports bars: Pinche Gringo BBQ, Black Horse English Pub.
- **England / Scotland** — Black Horse Pub (Roma Norte), Condesa Hipodromo. UK expat scene.
- **Germany** — small; Café Gourmand, German cultural center.
- **Korea Republic** — small expat community; Korean restaurants in Polanco and Zona Rosa.
- **Japan** — Polanco. Nicos, Edoko, izakayas.
- **France** — French expat scene strong in Polanco; bistros and cafés.
- **Portugal** — sparse but real.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Roma Norte / Condesa** — gentrified, cosmopolitan. Will show every match. Easy walk-in for non-marquee fixtures, packed for Mexico/marquee.
- **Centro Histórico** — older cantinas, more authentic Mexican supporter rooms.
- **Polanco** — affluent, expat-heavy. Best for European matches.
- **Coyoacán** — historic, intellectual, casual. Cantinas and cafés.
- **Coapa (south of city)** — Azteca-adjacent. Match-day chaos.

## Match-day logistics

- **Estadio Azteca:** Tren Ligero light rail to Estadio Azteca station. ~45 min from Centro.
- **Drive from Roma to Azteca:** 45–90 min depending on traffic. Match days = chaos.
- **Watching from Roma/Condesa on a Mexico match day:** every cantina in walking distance will be flagged green and packed. Walk in 90 min before kickoff.

## Weather

June/July CDMX: mild — 65–75°F, but rainy season. Afternoon thunderstorms common. Outdoor patios hit-or-miss.

## Booking advice

- Mexico matches: walk-in only at most cantinas, but reserve at sit-down restaurants in Polanco/Roma. Arrive 90 min before kickoff for prime walk-in.
- Marquee Mexico fixtures (vs Korea, vs Argentina if drawn): every Mexico-flagged spot reserves out 2 weeks early at sit-down restaurants.
- Non-Mexico matches: walk-in friendly across most of the city.

## Submit a venue

Cantinas off the gringo trail (working-class neighborhoods like Iztapalapa, Doctores, Tepito-adjacent) underrepresented. Submit them — they're the real ones.

# Dallas / Arlington — what watch parties feel like here

AT&T Stadium hosts nine matches — among the most of any host city. The DFW metro is sprawling and car-dependent, but its diaspora communities are large and well-organized. The Mexican community is enormous, the Latin American population growing, and Asian (Vietnamese, Korean, Indian) suburbs in Plano/Carrollton are the off-the-radar match-day rooms.

## How to think about DFW

Decide whether you're watching in Dallas proper, the suburbs, or Arlington/Fort Worth — they're three different scenes and you don't want to switch mid-day. Dallas proper is more sports-bar-driven. Suburbs are diaspora-driven and require a car.

## Where each crowd lives

- **USA** — every sports bar in Uptown, Deep Ellum, Lower Greenville. Stan's, Trinity Hall, The Standard.
- **Mexico** — Bishop Arts, Oak Cliff, Garland. Cantinas full from open. Dallas's Mexican supporter community is one of the largest in the US.
- **Argentina / South American** — sparse but real; small Argentine restaurants in Plano and Frisco.
- **Korea Republic** — Carrollton K-Town. KBBQ, soju bars, Korean cafes.
- **Japan** — Plano and Frisco have Japanese restaurants. Mr Sushi, Tei An.
- **England / Scotland** — Trinity Hall (Mockingbird Station), The Old Monk, Idle Rich. UK-pub scene is solid.
- **Germany** — Bavarian Grill (Plano), Kuby's (Snider Plaza). Smaller scene than NY/Chicago but vocal.
- **Brazil** — Texas de Brazil and a handful of churrascaria-style spots. Brazilian community in Irving.
- **Netherlands** — sparse.
- **Vietnam** (proxy for SE Asian watching) — Garland, Arlington, Carrollton Vietnamese neighborhoods will show JPN, KOR.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Bishop Arts (Oak Cliff)** — best Mexican supporter rooms.
- **Carrollton K-Town** — all KOR matches.
- **Uptown / Deep Ellum** — generic sports bars, USA matches.
- **Plano / Frisco** — Japanese, Korean, Indian, Pakistani; quiet but active for diaspora matches.
- **Arlington** — SoFi-adjacent... wait, AT&T-adjacent. Sports bars near the stadium fill on match days but are otherwise unremarkable.

## Match-day logistics

- AT&T parking is plentiful but slow on exit (60–90 min after final whistle). Tailgating culture from NFL crowds carries over.
- Driving from Dallas to Arlington: 35–50 min depending on traffic.
- TRE train Dallas–Fort Worth runs to Centreport, then bus to AT&T. Slower than driving; useful for fans in alcohol mode.

## Weather

June/July Texas — extreme heat, often 100°F+. Patios are unusable mid-afternoon. AC is mandatory. Indoor matches at AT&T are the rare comfort.

## Booking advice

- USA matches: any Uptown sports bar fills. Reserve.
- Mexico matches at AT&T: every Mexican-flagged spot in DFW will be packed. Reserve at sit-down restaurants; arrive 90 min early at cantinas.
- Korea-Czechia at AT&T: Carrollton bars at capacity. Book the larger ones (Sura, OK Cafe).

# Toronto — what watch parties feel like here

BMO Field hosts six matches including Canada's opener. Toronto is the most diverse host city in the world, hands down. Every community has its own neighborhood: Little Portugal, Little Italy (not playing), Little Greece (Danforth, not playing), Little Korea, Koreatown North, Little Jamaica, Brampton's Caribbean and South Asian, Scarborough's Sri Lankan and Chinese, Mississauga's Pakistani.

## How to think about Toronto

Pick the country, find the neighborhood, walk in. Toronto's diaspora rooms are some of the most authentic in North America — better in many cases than the home country's expat scene anywhere else. Streetcars and subway make this the easiest car-free host city.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Canada** — BMO Field-adjacent (Liberty Village), King West, Kensington Market. The Pour House, Bar Hop, Loaded Pierogi.
- **Portugal** — Little Portugal (Dundas West, Ossington). Café Diplomatico, Café Vienna. Tight, supporter-club style.
- **Italy** (not playing) — Little Italy (College). Bars will show neighbors.
- **Greece** (not playing) — Danforth. Bars will show.
- **Korea Republic** — Bloor Korea (between Christie and Bathurst), Korean Town North (Yonge & Sheppard). KBBQ rooms.
- **Brazil** — small but growing; Lula Lounge for cultural events, plus Brazilian restaurants.
- **Argentina** — sparse but loyal. Argentine restaurants in the West End.
- **Mexico** — small in Toronto proper; growing in Brampton and York region. El Catrin, La Carnita.
- **England / Scotland** — Madison Avenue Pub (UofT), The Pilot, Scallywags. Robust UK-pub scene.
- **Germany** — Bier Markt (multiple), Otto's Bierhalle.
- **Spain** — Bar Raval (tapas-driven, supporter-friendly).
- **Netherlands** — sparse.
- **Senegal / Ghana / West African** — Little Jamaica (Eglinton West), Brampton, Mississauga. Tight community.
- **Caribbean (Haiti, Cabo Verde indirectly)** — Little Jamaica, Scarborough, Brampton.
- **Iran / Persian** — North York, Richmond Hill (Persian community is the largest in North America). Persian restaurants and tea houses.
- **Pakistan / India** (not playing — proxy) — Mississauga, Brampton. Will show neighboring matches.
- **Bosnia and Herzegovina** — community in Etobicoke and Mississauga; supporter clubs.
- **Croatia** — older Croatian community in West End and suburbs.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Liberty Village / King West** — BMO Field-adjacent. Canada match HQ.
- **Little Portugal (Dundas West)** — Portugal matches are events here.
- **Bloor Koreatown / Yonge North** — Korea matches.
- **North York / Richmond Hill** — Persian community.
- **Brampton** — Caribbean, South Asian, growing African.
- **Scarborough** — Sri Lankan, Chinese, Filipino.
- **Etobicoke / Mississauga** — Bosnian, Croatian, Polish, Italian.

## Match-day logistics

- **BMO Field:** Liberty Village location. 509 King streetcar or short walk from Bathurst Station. Easiest stadium for transit in N America.
- **Pre-match:** Liberty Village and King West fill 3 hours pre-kickoff for Canada matches.
- **Subway from anywhere in old Toronto:** 20–30 min to BMO Field.

## Weather

June/July Toronto: 70–85°F, mostly sunny, some humidity. Patios usable. Long daylight hours.

## Booking advice

- Canada matches: Liberty Village/King West reserve out 5+ days early. Etobicoke and West End secondary.
- Portugal matches: Little Portugal cafes don't take reservations; arrive 90 min early.
- Korea matches: Bloor Koreatown KBBQ rooms reserve.
- Bosnia / Croatia matches: supporter clubs in Etobicoke handle their own door.

## Submit a venue

Sri Lankan, Bangladeshi, Liberian, Sudanese, Cape Verdean rooms in Brampton/Scarborough/Mississauga underrepresented. Toronto has the longest tail of any host city.

# Boston — what watch parties feel like here

Gillette hosts seven matches. Boston's strength is its concentrated immigrant communities — Brazilian (Framingham, Allston), Irish (everywhere), Haitian (Mattapan, Brockton), Cape Verdean (Brockton, Dorchester), Salvadoran (East Boston). Irish pubs are everywhere and will reliably show England, Scotland, Ireland (not playing — pubs will show neighbors instead).

## How to think about Boston

Boston's metro is small but its diaspora communities are concentrated and loyal. The Cape Verdean community in Brockton is the largest in the world outside Cabo Verde itself. The Brazilian community in Framingham (the "Brazil of New England") will be at full volume for any Brazil match.

## Where each crowd lives

- **USA** — Cask 'n Flagon, Fenway pubs, Boston Beer Works. Generic sports bars across Allston and Brighton.
- **Brazil** — Framingham (the largest), Allston, East Boston. Brazilian restaurants, churrascarias, and grocery cafes show every Brazil match.
- **Cabo Verde** — Brockton, Dorchester. The world's most concentrated Cape Verdean diaspora. Cape Verdean American Veterans Hall, restaurants on Main St Brockton.
- **Haiti** — Mattapan, Brockton. Haitian restaurants and bakeries. Le Foyer.
- **Argentina** — small but loyal. La Voile, Argentine restaurants in suburbs.
- **Mexico** — East Boston, Chelsea, Lynn. Cantinas and Mexican restaurants.
- **Salvador / Honduras** — East Boston, Chelsea. Will show LATAM matches generally.
- **England** — every Irish pub plus dedicated UK pubs. Phoenix Landing (Cambridge), Dillon's (Back Bay), Solas.
- **Scotland** — Hennessy's, Mr Dooley's. Same pubs as English fans usually.
- **Ireland** (not playing) — full Irish-American pub scene; pubs default to nearest neighbor.
- **Germany** — Bukowski Tavern, Jacob Wirth (closed unfortunately) — German scene smaller.
- **Korea Republic** — Cambridge, Allston, Burlington. Wonsuk, Jin Korean BBQ.
- **Japan** — small; Kaze, izakayas in Allston.
- **Senegal / Ghana** — small communities in Roxbury and Mattapan; supporter clubs more than bars.
- **Portugal** — East Boston, Cambridge. Portuguese restaurants and bakeries. Important for any Portugal match.
- **IR Iran** — Brookline. Persian restaurants.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Allston / Brighton** — Brazilian, Korean, generic college sports bars.
- **Mattapan / Brockton** — Haitian, Cape Verdean. Tight communities.
- **East Boston / Chelsea / Revere** — Salvadoran, Mexican, Italian-American.
- **Framingham** — Brazilian. Drive 25 min from Boston for the most authentic Brazil-match room.
- **Cambridge / Somerville** — diverse, multiple sports bars, college-age crowd.
- **South Boston** — Irish-American pubs default to nearest neighbor when Ireland isn't playing.

## Match-day logistics

- **Gillette Stadium:** in Foxborough, 30 miles south of Boston. Commuter Rail runs special trains on event days from South Station to Foxboro Station, ~50 min. Driving: I-95 South, ~45 min, $50+ parking.
- **Cape Verde match:** Brockton clubs and restaurants — drive 20–30 min south of Boston.
- **Brazil match:** drive to Framingham (25 min west) or stay in Allston for the city version.
- **Final/quarterfinal:** every UK pub and supporter bar in Boston books out.

## Weather

June/July: 75–85°F, humid. Summer thunderstorms common. Patios usable.

## Booking advice

- Cape Verde and Haiti matches: Brockton/Mattapan restaurants don't take reservations; arrive early. Restaurant rooms fill 90 min pre-kickoff.
- Brazil matches: Framingham restaurants take reservations for groups of 6+. Allston walk-in.
- USA matches: Fenway-area pubs reserve.
- England/Scotland: Cambridge and Back Bay UK pubs reserve 3+ days out for marquee matches.

## Submit a venue

Salvadoran, Honduran, Iraqi, Angolan rooms in Brockton/Mattapan/Chelsea underrepresented. Submit them.

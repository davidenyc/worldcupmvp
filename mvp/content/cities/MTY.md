# Monterrey — what watch parties feel like here

Monterrey hosts a handful of matches. Northern Mexico's industrial capital, MTY has a strong professional-class football culture (Rayados, Tigres) and a heavy USA-Mexico crossover for Mexican-American fans coming down for matches. Cantinas, carne asada spots, and northern Mexican parrillas will all be loud.

## How to think about MTY

Centro and Barrio Antiguo for cantinas and traditional Mexican supporter rooms. San Pedro Garza García (the wealthy suburb) for affluent, English-speaking, multi-screen sports bars. Driving everywhere — MTY is car-dependent.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Mexico** — everywhere. Cantinas in Centro, Barrio Antiguo, every neighborhood. MTY is regio territory.
- **USA expats / Mexican-American visitors** — San Pedro, Garza García. American sports bars: Hooters, Buffalo Wild Wings (yes), Tomatlán.
- **Argentina / Spain** — Spanish-themed parrillas in San Pedro and Cumbres.
- **Korea / Japan** — small expat community; Asian restaurants in San Pedro.
- **England** — Sherlock Holmes Pub, British expat spots.
- **France** — French expat scene small but present in San Pedro.
- **Germany** — German cultural center, Auto industry expat community.
- **Brazil** — small Brazilian-Mexican community.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Centro / Barrio Antiguo** — historic. Cantinas, traditional supporter rooms.
- **San Pedro Garza García** — wealthy, English-speaking, multi-screen sports bars.
- **Cumbres** — middle-class Monterrey, neighborhood cantinas.

## Match-day logistics

- **Stadium:** Uber/drive. Match-day shuttles run.
- **From Centro to stadium:** 30 min.
- **From San Pedro:** 25 min.

## Weather

June/July MTY: hot — 90–100°F. Outdoor venues hit-or-miss. Heavy AC mandatory.

## Booking advice

- Mexico matches: cantinas walk-in. San Pedro sports bars reserve.
- Non-Mexico matches: walk-in friendly.

## Submit a venue

Local Monterrey cantinas and birrierías in Apodaca and Cumbres underrepresented. Submit them.

# Seattle — what watch parties feel like here

Lumen Field hosts six matches. Seattle has the most concentrated soccer culture in the US thanks to Sounders FC — bars know what supporter culture looks like. Pioneer Square is the supporter neighborhood. Diaspora communities are smaller than NY/LA but tightly organized: Ethiopian, Eritrean, Mexican, Korean, Japanese, Vietnamese.

## How to think about Seattle

Pioneer Square first. It's walkable from Lumen Field, full of pre-existing supporter pubs (Fuel, Pyramid, Cheers!), and on a match day every spot will be flagged for the relevant nation. For diaspora-specific matches, head to specific neighborhoods (International District for Asian, Beacon Hill for African, Bellevue for Persian/Indian).

## Where each crowd lives

- **USA** — Pioneer Square (Sounders supporter culture). Pyramid Brewery, FX McRory's, Fuel.
- **Mexico** — South Park, White Center, Burien. Cantinas full for any Mexico match.
- **Korea Republic** — International District, Bellevue, Federal Way. KBBQ rooms with screens.
- **Japan** — Uwajimaya area, Bellevue. Izakayas and ramen spots.
- **Vietnam** (not playing but proxy for SE Asian) — Little Saigon (CID), South King County.
- **IR Iran** — Bellevue, Redmond. Persian restaurants and tea houses.
- **Senegal / West African** — Beacon Hill, Rainier Valley. Senegalese, Eritrean, Ethiopian community overlap.
- **Ghana** — Beacon Hill, Federal Way. Tight community.
- **South Africa** — small, scattered. Capitol Hill bars sometimes host.
- **Brazil** — small Brazilian community in Capitol Hill and Ballard. Casa Brasil.
- **Argentina** — sparse. Patagón (Bellevue) takes the matches.
- **England / Scotland** — George & Dragon (Fremont), Owl 'n' Thistle (downtown). Solid UK-pub scene.
- **Germany** — Feierabend (South Lake Union), Prost! (Phinney Ridge). Beer-hall culture is real.
- **Spain** — limited. The Hi-Life and a few tapas spots.
- **Netherlands** — sparse.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Pioneer Square** — supporter HQ for any USA match and most marquee matches. Lumen Field-adjacent.
- **Capitol Hill** — diverse, multiple sports bars, generic-good for any match.
- **International District (CID)** — Korean, Japanese, Vietnamese, Chinese.
- **Beacon Hill / Rainier Valley** — Ethiopian, Eritrean, Senegalese, Ghanaian.
- **Bellevue / Eastside** — Persian, Indian, tech-driven international crowd.
- **Ballard** — Scandinavian heritage; some Norway support but mostly generic Pacific NW bars.

## Match-day logistics

- **Lumen Field:** Link Light Rail from anywhere on the line — easiest stadium of the tournament. Walk to Pioneer Square pre/post.
- **Pre-match:** Pioneer Square fills 2 hours pre-kickoff. Post-match: same area for hours.
- **From Bellevue/Eastside:** drive to South Bellevue station, train in.
- **USA-Australia:** Pioneer Square will be standing-room. Reserve at sit-down spots.

## Weather

June/July Seattle: 65–75°F, mostly dry, sometimes drizzle. Patios usable. Long daylight hours.

## Booking advice

- USA matches: Pioneer Square bars reserve out 5+ days early. Capitol Hill is backup.
- IRN matches: Bellevue restaurants take reservations; do it.
- Mexico matches: South Park / White Center cantinas walk-in.
- Marquee East African match (Senegal, Egypt): Beacon Hill restaurants book up fast.

Eritrean, Ethiopian (despite not playing — important diaspora rooms), Salvadoran rooms in South King County underrepresented.

# Houston — what watch parties feel like here

NRG Stadium hosts seven matches. Houston is the most diverse host city by some metrics — Mexican, Salvadoran, Honduran, Vietnamese, Indian/Pakistani, Nigerian, Arab, Iranian, Korean, Chinese, Ethiopian communities are all enormous. If a country has a North American diaspora, Houston probably has 100,000+ of them.

## How to think about HOU

Decide which corridor: Hillcroft (Mahatma Gandhi District) for South Asian/Pakistani, Bellaire for Chinese/Vietnamese/Korean, Spring Branch for Salvadoran/Honduran, Sharpstown for Latin American working-class, EaDo/Midtown for generic-cosmopolitan. Drive 30–45 min between any two.

## Where each crowd lives

- **USA** — Midtown, Heights, EaDo. Pitch 25, Lucky's Pub, Truck Yard.
- **Mexico** — Sharpstown, East End, Pasadena. Cantinas. Cantina La Adelita, El Tiempo Cantina (multiple locations).
- **El Salvador / Honduras / Guatemala** — Spring Branch, Long Point Road. Will show LATAM matches generally.
- **Brazil** — small but real Brazilian community in Sugar Land.
- **Argentina** — sparse; a few Argentine restaurants in Galleria area.
- **Korea Republic** — Spring Branch and Bellaire. KBBQ rooms.
- **Japan** — Westchase / Town & Country. Izakayas and ramen.
- **Vietnam** (not playing — proxy) — Bellaire/Chinatown, Asiatown. Will show JPN, KOR.
- **Senegal / Ghana / Nigeria** — Hillcroft, Alief, Westchase. Nigerian-American population in Houston is one of the largest in the US.
- **South Africa** — small, scattered.
- **IR Iran** — Westchase, Galleria area. Persian restaurants.
- **Saudi Arabia / Jordan / UAE** — Hillcroft, Sugar Land. Arab community.
- **Pakistan / India** (not playing — proxy) — Hillcroft (Mahatma Gandhi District). Will show neighboring matches.
- **England / Scotland** — Black Labrador Pub, Brixton Public House.
- **Germany** — King's Biergarten (Pearland), Spring Sausage.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Hillcroft (Mahatma Gandhi District)** — South Asian, Arab, Persian. Multiple flag-aware restaurants.
- **Bellaire / Chinatown / Asiatown** — Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean. Karaoke bars and KBBQ.
- **Spring Branch / Long Point** — Salvadoran, Honduran, Korean.
- **East End / Pasadena** — Mexican.
- **Sharpstown** — working-class Latin American.
- **Sugar Land** — Indian, Brazilian, Persian (suburban affluent).

## Match-day logistics

- **NRG Stadium:** METRORail Red Line stops at NRG Park. ~20 min from downtown.
- **Pre-match:** parking lots fill 2 hours before. Tailgating exists.
- **From Hillcroft / Bellaire to NRG:** 25–35 min drive.

## Weather

June/July Houston: brutal heat and humidity, 95°F+ with high humidity. Outdoor venues unusable mid-day. AC mandatory.

## Booking advice

- Germany match (NRG): Pearland's King's Biergarten reserves out fast.
- Portugal-Congo DR (NRG): small Portuguese community in Sugar Land; restaurants walk-in.
- Mexico matches: Sharpstown and Pasadena cantinas walk-in.
- Marquee African match (Senegal, Ghana, Nigeria proxy): Hillcroft restaurants don't reserve; arrive early.

## Submit a venue

Nigerian, Liberian, Ghanaian, Salvadoran, Honduran, Pakistani-aware, and Vietnamese rooms underrepresented. Houston needs the most submissions.

# Miami — what watch parties feel like here

Miami is the most Latin American host city. Hard Rock Stadium hosts seven matches. Argentine, Brazilian, Colombian, Venezuelan, Cuban-American, Haitian, and Russian-Israeli communities are all enormous and vocal. If your match has a Latin American team, Miami will be loud — and Argentine bars fill 6 hours pre-kickoff for big matches.

## How to think about Miami

Pick by neighborhood, not by venue type. Doral is Venezuelan/Argentine. Allapattah is Colombian. Little Havana is Cuban (which means they'll show every Latin American team). Little Haiti shows Haiti matches and has its own rhythm. Brickell and Wynwood are generic-cosmopolitan.

## Where each crowd lives

- **Argentina** — Doral, Sunny Isles. Argentine parrillas, often Argentine-Uruguayan crossover. Graziano's, La Cabaña, La Carreta (with caveats — La Carreta is more Cuban).
- **Brazil** — small but growing in Sunny Isles and Aventura. Boteco, Lapa.
- **Uruguay** — Miami and Hudson County NJ are the two biggest US Uruguayan rooms. Loyal at parrillas.
- **Colombia** — Allapattah and Doral. Steakhouses and arepa-driven spots.
- **Venezuela** (not playing but proxies for LATAM watching) — Doral specifically.
- **Cuba** (not playing, proxy for LATAM watching) — Little Havana, Hialeah. Cuban cafes will show every Latin match. Versailles is iconic.
- **Mexico** — smaller in Miami than other host cities; Doral and Hialeah have spots.
- **Haiti** — Little Haiti (Lemon City). Tap Tap, Chef Creole. Haiti's matches are events.
- **USA** — Brickell, Wynwood, Miami Beach. Chico's, American Social.
- **Saudi Arabia / Jordan** — Sunny Isles, parts of Coral Gables (Russian-Israeli-Arab crossover).
- **South Korea** — small. Aventura and Brickell have a few Korean spots.
- **England** — Tobacco Road, John Martin's (Coral Gables).
- **Germany** — small; Fritz & Franz Bierhaus (Coral Gables).
- **Spain** — limited; Boqueria-style spots in Brickell.
- **Senegal / West African** — small, scattered.
- **Russia / Eastern Europe** (not playing) — Sunny Isles is a Russian-Israeli enclave; some bars may show specific matches anyway.

## Neighborhoods worth knowing

- **Doral** — Argentine, Venezuelan, Colombian. Where most LATAM matches are watched.
- **Little Havana** — Cuban cafes and casual restaurants. Will show every LATAM match.
- **Allapattah** — Colombian central.
- **Sunny Isles / Aventura** — Argentine, Brazilian, Russian-Israeli.
- **Little Haiti** — Haiti matches are here, full stop.
- **Brickell / Wynwood** — generic sports bars. USA, England, Western European matches.
- **Hialeah** — Cuban-American, Latin American. Very local.

## Match-day logistics

- **Hard Rock Stadium:** in Miami Gardens, north of the city. Drive (no rapid transit). 30–45 min from downtown.
- **From Miami Beach:** plan an hour each way.
- **Argentina matches at Hard Rock:** Doral cantinas full from 6 hours pre-kickoff. Reserve at sit-down restaurants.
- **From Fort Lauderdale:** Brightline to Miami Central station, then drive/Lyft.

## Weather

June/July: hot, humid, afternoon thunderstorms. Outdoor venues hit-or-miss. Air conditioning is mandatory.

## Booking advice

- Argentina/Brazil/Uruguay matches: reserve at Doral or Sunny Isles parrillas a week ahead for marquee fixtures.
- Mexico matches: Doral and Hialeah cantinas walk-in; arrive early.
- Haiti matches: Little Haiti restaurants don't take reservations, walk-in. The room is the point.
- Final at MetLife: Miami's Argentine/Brazilian rooms still pack out for the watch.

## Submit a venue

Salvadoran, Honduran, Ghanaian, Senegalese rooms underrepresented in Miami. We want them.

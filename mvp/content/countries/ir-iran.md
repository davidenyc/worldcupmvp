# IR Iran

Persian football culture is musical, devoted, and centered on Tehrangeles (LA's Westwood). The largest Iranian diaspora outside Iran. Persian restaurants and tea houses become packed match-day rooms.

## How IR Iran fans show up

Persian restaurants, kebab houses, and tea houses are the home rooms. Westwood's Tehrangeles is the densest concentration in the world. Toronto's North York/Richmond Hill is the largest in Canada. Tea, kebab, Persian music.

## Where to find the crowd

- **LA (Tehrangeles/Westwood):** Shaherzad, Shamshiri, Cafe Glace.
- **NYC:** Persepolis (UES), Shiraz Kitchen & Wine Bar (Westchester), Pars Grill House.
- **DC:** Persian restaurants in NoVa and Tysons.
- **Houston:** Westchase Persian restaurants.
- **Toronto:** North York and Richmond Hill — multiple options.
- **Vancouver:** North Vancouver Lonsdale corridor — Persian central in Canada.

## Match-day rituals

- Tea (chai) — black tea with sugar cubes between teeth.
- Kebab and saffron rice.
- Persian music between halves.
- Drums and "Iran! Iran!" chants.
- No alcohol at most spots.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Strong recent form.
- Tehrangeles will be a national-attention room for any Iran match.
- Group stage with New Zealand at SoFi.

## What to wear

Red, white, green. Iran shirt or scarf.

## Bring

Respect for the cafés. Don't expect alcohol; do expect tea, kebab, saffron rice.

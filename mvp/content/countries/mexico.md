# Mexico

Co-host. Largest single supporter culture in this tournament by raw numbers. El Tri matches are city-wide events in Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey, AND in every city in the US with a Mexican-American community (which is most of them).

## How Mexico fans show up

Cantinas, taquerías, birrierías, and Mexican restaurants. Walk into any Mexican-owned spot in any city in North America during a Mexico match and you'll find the room. Drumming, mariachi, "Cielito Lindo" and the inevitable goal celebration of "Mexico, Mexico, Mexico."

## Where to find the crowd

- **Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey:** every cantina. Every taquería. Every neighborhood.
- **LA (Boyle Heights, East LA, San Fernando):** countless cantinas.
- **Houston (Sharpstown, East End):** dozens of options.
- **Dallas (Bishop Arts, Oak Cliff):** multiple cantinas.
- **NYC:** Cantina Rooftop, Cantina Taqueria locations, La Cantina, H&H Cantina, 345 Cantina.
- **Chicago:** Pilsen, Little Village.
- **Atlanta (Buford Highway):** Plaza Fiesta and corridor restaurants.
- **Toronto:** El Catrin, La Carnita, Tres Carnales.
- **Phoenix, San Diego, every host:** Mexican supporter rooms in every neighborhood.

## Match-day rituals

- Tequila and beer (Modelo, Pacifico, Corona).
- Tacos, birria, carne asada.
- Mariachi between halves at sit-down restaurants.
- "Cielito Lindo" sung at any moment of joy.
- "Mexico, Mexico, Mexico" chant on goals.
- "Pacheco" cheer (the call-and-response with the goalkeeper).

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Co-host opening the tournament at the Azteca vs South Africa.
- 80,000+ at the Azteca for that match.
- Group stage with Korea Republic — a marquee fixture.
- Pressure of host status.

## What to wear

Green, white, red. Mexico shirt — any era. Sombrero optional but loved.

## Bring

Energy. Mexico rooms are full-volume affairs.

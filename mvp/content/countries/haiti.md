# Haiti

A historic qualification (Haiti's first WC since 1974). The Haitian-American community in Miami's Little Haiti, Boston's Brockton/Mattapan, NYC's Flatbush, and Toronto's North York will be at full volume for every match.

## How Haiti fans show up

Haitian restaurants, kompa music venues, and church halls are the home rooms. Multi-generational, family-oriented, drumming and singing.

## Where to find the crowd

- **Miami (Little Haiti, Lemon City):** Tap Tap, Chef Creole, Buffet Crémas.
- **Boston (Mattapan, Brockton):** Le Foyer Bakery, Sufficient Soul Restaurant.
- **NYC (Brooklyn):** Flatbush, East Flatbush, Crown Heights Haitian restaurants.
- **NJ:** Hudson County Haitian community.
- **Toronto:** Haitian restaurants in North York and Scarborough.

## Match-day rituals

- Kompa music (Haitian dance music).
- Griot (fried pork) and pikliz (spicy pickled slaw).
- Blue and red flags.
- Multi-generational rooms — grandmothers and toddlers in the same room.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- First WC since 1974 — historic.
- Group stage with Scotland at Gillette Stadium and Brazil at LFF — both diaspora-event matches.

## What to wear

Blue and red. Haiti shirt. Haitian flag bandana.

## Bring

Joy. The Haitian community has waited 50+ years for this.

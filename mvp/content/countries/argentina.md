# Argentina

The defending champions. Loudest, most organized supporter culture in this tournament. If Argentina is playing, the room will sing.

## How Argentina fans show up

Steakhouses (parrillas) are the home base — Argentine-Uruguayan parrillas across the US double as supporter clubs during big tournaments. Drumming, flag-draped tables, "Muchachos" sung at every break. The Argentine community in Miami's Doral is the loudest concentration in North America. NYC, LA, and Mexico City all have well-known parrilla rooms.

## Where to find the crowd

- **Miami (Doral, Sunny Isles):** Graziano's, La Cabaña, multiple parrillas. The room.
- **NYC:** Palermo Bistro (Gramercy), Parrilla Steak House, Buenos Aires (East Village).
- **LA:** Carlitos Gardel, Patagón.
- **Mexico City:** Polanco parrillas argentinas.
- **Toronto:** Argentine restaurants in West End and Little Italy.
- **Houston, Dallas:** smaller scenes; Argentine restaurants in suburbs.

## Match-day rituals

- Drums and the *bombo legüero*. Expect them.
- "Muchachos" (the 2022 anthem) sung between halves and at any moment of joy.
- White-and-light-blue flags everywhere. Wear the colors.
- Steaks and Malbec are food. Take them seriously.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Defending the 2022 title with Messi likely playing his last World Cup.
- Strong bookies favorite alongside Brazil and France.
- Expect a deep run — group stage is a formality for any Argentine watch party.

## What to wear

Light blue and white. The Albiceleste shirt — any year, any era. If you don't have one, white shirt + light blue stripes.

## Bring

Argentine fan bars don't usually need anything from you, but bringing a small flag for the table earns nods.

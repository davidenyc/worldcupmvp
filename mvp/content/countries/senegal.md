# Senegal

The Lions of Teranga. African Cup of Nations winners (2022). One of the loudest, most musical supporter cultures in the tournament. The Senegalese-American community in NYC's Harlem (the Senegalese capital of the US) and the Bronx is enormous.

## How Senegal fans show up

Senegalese restaurants, drumming groups, and Wolof-speaking community spaces are home rooms. Harlem's Frederick Douglass Boulevard corridor has multiple Senegalese restaurants. Drumming, dancing, mbalax music.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC (Harlem):** Le Baobab, Pikine Restaurant, multiple options on Frederick Douglass Blvd.
- **NYC (Bronx):** West African corridor on White Plains Road.
- **Boston:** Roxbury and Mattapan Senegalese spots.
- **DC area:** Senegalese restaurants in NoVa.
- **Montreal (proxy):** Senegalese and West African restaurants.
- **Toronto:** West African community in Brampton and North York.

## Match-day rituals

- Mbalax music (Youssou N'Dour, Ismael Lo).
- Thieboudienne (national fish-and-rice dish).
- Bissap (hibiscus drink).
- Drumming, dancing.
- Wolof and French flowing.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Strong recent African form.
- Group stage with France at MetLife — a marquee NYC moment.
- Group stage with Norway at MetLife.

## What to wear

Green, yellow, red. Senegal shirt. Lion-imagery scarf.

## Bring

Energy. Senegalese rooms are full-volume.

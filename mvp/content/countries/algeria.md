# Algeria

A loud and proud North African football culture. Algeria's supporter rooms are some of the most underrated in the tournament — French-speaking, mint-tea-driven, and devoted.

## How Algeria fans show up

North African cafés and shisha lounges are the home base. The Algerian-American community in NYC's Astoria, Boston's Quincy/Allston, and Detroit's North End picks specific cafés and stays loyal. Often shared rooms with Moroccan and Tunisian fans for African matches generally.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC:** North African cafés in Astoria. Some Brooklyn spots.
- **Boston:** Allston and Quincy North African coffee houses.
- **Detroit:** Hamtramck and North End cafés.
- **Montreal (proxy — closest large city to NA host cities):** Plateau and Côte-des-Neiges.

## Match-day rituals

- Mint tea between halves.
- Green-and-white scarves.
- French and Arabic flowing simultaneously.
- Shared rooms with Moroccan/Tunisian fans for African solidarity matches.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Returning to the World Cup after multiple cycles away.
- Mahrez-led generation aging; new faces emerging.
- Strong group-stage threat to bigger names.

## What to wear

Green and white. Algeria scarf or shirt. If you don't have one, plain white shirt works.

## Bring

Respect the café rules. Don't expect alcohol; do expect mint tea, pastries, and full attention to the screen.

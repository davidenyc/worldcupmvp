# Bosnia and Herzegovina

One of the most underrated supporter cultures in the tournament. The Bosnian diaspora — particularly in St. Louis (the largest Bosnian-American population), Toronto, and Chicago — is fiercely loyal, organized, and loud.

## How Bosnia fans show up

Bosnian-American social clubs, restaurants, and dedicated fan bars are the home rooms. St. Louis's Bevo Mill / South City neighborhood has multiple Bosnian-owned spots that pack the room for any BIH match. Toronto's Etobicoke and Mississauga have similar concentrations.

## Where to find the crowd

- **St. Louis (closest to KC host city):** Bosnian restaurants and clubs in Bevo Mill / South City. The largest Bosnian-American community in the US.
- **NYC:** Bosnian community spread; Astoria has small spots.
- **Chicago (closest to KC, ATL):** Bosnian-American clubs in northern suburbs.
- **Toronto:** Etobicoke, Mississauga Bosnian-Canadian restaurants and supporter clubs.
- **Vancouver:** small but present.

## Match-day rituals

- Cevapi and somun (Bosnian sausages and bread). Coffee.
- Blue and yellow flags.
- "Bosnia, Bosnia" chants.
- Family-style watching — multi-generational in the room.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- BIH's second WC ever (first since 2014).
- Edin Džeko emotional last hurrah.
- Group stage with Canada at BMO Field is a marquee diaspora moment in Toronto.

## What to wear

Blue and yellow. BIH shirt. Bosnian flag pin.

## Bring

Respect. This is a community that does not take qualification for granted.

# Ghana

The Black Stars. One of the loudest African supporter cultures, especially in NYC's Bronx, Toronto's Brampton, and DC. Highlife and Afrobeats music.

## How Ghana fans show up

Ghanaian restaurants, social clubs, and church halls are the home rooms. The Bronx's Ghanaian community is one of the largest in the US. Toronto's Brampton has the largest Ghanaian-Canadian population. Drumming, highlife music, jollof rice.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC (Bronx):** Ghanaian restaurants on White Plains Road and Boston Road. Buka, Ginnah's Cuisine.
- **DC area:** Ghanaian restaurants in MD/VA suburbs.
- **Atlanta:** Decatur, Clarkston, Lithonia.
- **Houston:** Ghanaian community in southwest Houston.
- **Toronto (Brampton):** Multiple Ghanaian restaurants, social clubs.

## Match-day rituals

- Highlife music, Afrobeats.
- Jollof rice (and jollof debates with Senegalese fans).
- Red, gold, green flags.
- Drumming.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Group stage with Panama at BMO Field — a major Toronto diaspora event.
- Strong qualifying run.

## What to wear

Red, yellow, green. Ghana shirt — or a kente-cloth scarf.

## Bring

Patience for jollof debates with Senegal/Nigeria fans. They're cultural, not literal.

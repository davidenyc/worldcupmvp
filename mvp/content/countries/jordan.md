# Jordan

Historic first World Cup. The Jordanian-American community in Detroit, NYC, Chicago is small but devoted. Levantine cuisine, Arabic music, full cultural attention.

## How Jordan fans show up

Levantine restaurants — Jordanian, Lebanese, Palestinian crossover — are the home rooms. NYC's Levantine (Manhattan), Detroit's Dearborn corridor, Chicago's southwest suburbs.

## Where to find the crowd

- **Detroit (Dearborn area):** Levantine restaurants — overlap with Lebanese, Palestinian, Iraqi communities.
- **NYC:** Levantine (a literal name, listed in the app), Tanoreen (Bay Ridge).
- **Chicago:** SW suburbs Jordanian-Palestinian community.
- **LA:** Levantine restaurants in Anaheim and OC.
- **Toronto:** small Jordanian-Canadian community in Mississauga.

## Match-day rituals

- Mansaf (Jordanian national dish — lamb with yogurt sauce on rice).
- Tea and Turkish coffee.
- Arabic music between halves.
- Multi-generational rooms.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- First-ever WC qualification — historic.
- Group stage with Austria at Levi's Stadium.

## What to wear

Black, white, green, red. Jordan shirt or scarf. Keffiyeh optional.

## Bring

Respect for the cuisine. Mansaf is the point — try it.

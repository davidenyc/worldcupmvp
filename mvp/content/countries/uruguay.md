# Uruguay

La Celeste. Two-time World Cup champions (1930, 1950). The Uruguayan-American community is concentrated in Miami and Hudson County NJ — often overlapping with Argentine fan rooms at parrillas (Argentine-Uruguayan steakhouses).

## How Uruguay fans show up

Argentine-Uruguayan parrillas (steakhouses) are home rooms. Loyal supporter base — proportionally one of the loudest in the tournament.

## Where to find the crowd

- **Miami:** Argentine-Uruguayan parrillas in Doral and Sunny Isles.
- **NYC:** Argentine restaurants double as Uruguayan rooms; Hudson County NJ similarly.
- **LA:** small.
- **Mexico City:** Uruguayan-Argentine parrillas in Polanco and Roma.

## Match-day rituals

- Mate (passed around the table — never refuse, never wash the mate).
- Asado (Uruguayan grill — beef, sausages, sweetbreads).
- Tannat red wine.
- "Ole Ole Ole, Uruguay!" chants.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Marcelo Bielsa as manager — fascinating tactical lens.
- Strong CONMEBOL form.
- Group stage with Saudi Arabia at Hard Rock and Cabo Verde at Hard Rock.

## What to wear

Sky blue. Uruguay shirt. Sky blue scarves.

## Bring

A willingness to drink mate. Don't refuse the gourd.

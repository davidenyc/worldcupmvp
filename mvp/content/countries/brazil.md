# Brazil

Five-time champions. The most musical, danceable, and joyful supporter culture in the tournament. Brazilian rooms are events — drumming, dancing, samba breaks at half time.

## How Brazil fans show up

Brazilian restaurants, churrascarias, and Brazilian-owned bars are the home base. The Brazilian community in Framingham/Boston ("Brazil of New England") and Newark Ironbound are the densest in the US. NYC's Little Brazil (W 46th St) and Miami's Sunny Isles also strong.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC:** Emporium Brasil (Midtown), Via Brasil, Beco (Brooklyn). Little Brazil block on W 46th.
- **Boston / Framingham:** Brazilian restaurants on Concord St and Waverly. Many.
- **Newark Ironbound:** dozens of Brazilian-Portuguese establishments.
- **Miami (Sunny Isles, Aventura):** Boteco, Lapa, Brazilian churrascarias.
- **LA:** Bossa Nova locations, Picanha Churrascaria.
- **Toronto:** Lula Lounge, Brazilian restaurants in West End.
- **Mexico City:** Polanco and Roma Brazilian restaurants.

## Match-day rituals

- Drums (samba style). Often live drumming during matches.
- Dancing between halves.
- Caipirinhas, brahma beer, picanha.
- "Eu sou brasileiro com muito orgulho" — common chant.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Carlo Ancelotti coaching Brazil for the first time.
- Vinicius Jr. and Endrick the new core.
- High expectations after the 2022 quarterfinal exit.

## What to wear

Yellow, green, blue. Brazil shirt — any era. Yellow shorts, green socks.

## Bring

A willingness to dance. Brazilian rooms don't sit still during halftime.

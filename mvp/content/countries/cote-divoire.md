# Cote d'Ivoire

The Elephants are West African football royalty. The Ivorian diaspora in NYC's Bronx, Harlem, DC, and Toronto is loud and confident.

## How Cote d'Ivoire fans show up

Ivorian and West African restaurants, and shared rooms with Ghanaian, Senegalese, Nigerian fans for African solidarity matches. NYC's Harlem and Bronx have multiple Ivorian-owned restaurants.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC:** Le Baobab (Harlem), Senegalese-Ivorian restaurants on Frederick Douglass Blvd. Bronx west African corridor.
- **DC area (proxy):** Ivorian restaurants in NoVa.
- **Toronto:** West African community in Brampton and North York.
- **Atlanta:** Decatur, Clarkston communities.

## Match-day rituals

- Coupé-décalé and zouglou music.
- Attiéké (cassava couscous) and grilled fish.
- Orange shirts.
- Drumming.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Reigning Africa Cup of Nations champions.
- Group stage with Ecuador at Lincoln Financial Field.

## What to wear

Orange, white, green. Cote d'Ivoire shirt. Orange dominant.

## Bring

Patience for the lengthy meals between matches. The food is the point.

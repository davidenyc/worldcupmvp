# Curacao

A first-ever World Cup appearance for the smallest nation by population to ever qualify. The Curaçaoan diaspora — concentrated in NYC, Miami, and Toronto — will be celebrating one of the great qualification stories in tournament history.

## How Curacao fans show up

Caribbean restaurants, social clubs, and church halls are the home rooms. The Curaçaoan community in NYC's Brooklyn and Manhattan, plus parts of NJ, will pick specific bars. Tumba and tambú music.

## Where to find the crowd

- **NYC:** Caribbean restaurants in Brooklyn (Crown Heights, Flatbush). Specific Curaçaoan-owned spots.
- **Miami:** Caribbean Aventura and Sunny Isles spots.
- **Toronto:** Caribbean-Canadian community in Scarborough and Brampton.
- **Houston:** small but real.

## Match-day rituals

- Tumba and tambú music.
- Multi-generational rooms.
- Caribbean-style food and rum.
- Dutch + Papiamentu spoken interchangeably.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Historic first qualification — every match is a celebration.
- Group stage matches in NRG Houston (vs Germany) and Arrowhead (vs Ecuador).

## What to wear

Blue and yellow. Curacao shirt. Caribbean flags optional but loved.

## Bring

Joy. The community has waited forever.

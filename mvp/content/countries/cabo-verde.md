# Cabo Verde

A first-ever World Cup appearance. The Cape Verdean diaspora in Boston (Brockton specifically) is the largest concentration of Cabo Verdeans anywhere — bigger than any single city in Cabo Verde itself. The energy is going to be enormous.

## How Cabo Verde fans show up

Cape Verdean restaurants, social clubs, and church halls are the home rooms. Brockton's Main St corridor has multiple options. Music — funaná, batuque, morna — between halves.

## Where to find the crowd

- **Boston (Brockton):** Cape Verdean restaurants and clubs along Main St. The Cape Verdean American Veterans Hall is a known venue.
- **Boston (Dorchester):** Restaurants on Bowdoin St.
- **NYC (Brockton overflow):** Cape Verdean spots in Crown Heights and Brooklyn.
- **Providence/Pawtucket RI (proxy):** Strong CV community, will travel to Boston for marquee matches.

## Match-day rituals

- Funaná and morna music between halves.
- Cachupa (the national dish) often served.
- Grogue (Cape Verdean rum) for celebrations.
- Multi-generational rooms.

## Tournament storylines (2026)

- Historic first qualification. Every match is a celebration.
- Cabo Verde's national football culture has exploded recently.
- Group stage matches will be community-wide events in Brockton/Dorchester.

## What to wear

Blue, white, red. Cabo Verde shirt. Blue and white scarves.

## Bring

Joy. This is a community celebrating something they've waited for forever.
